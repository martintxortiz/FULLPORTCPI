## What the Framework Gets Right

The multi-filter entry stack (MA alignment + ADX + strong candle + RSI pullback + proximity to MA50) drastically reduces false signal frequency ‚Äî a genuine edge in volatile markets where noise dominates. ATR-normalized stops and ATR-based position sizing are the right approach because they scale with realized volatility rather than fixed percentages, keeping your risk-per-trade stable across regimes. The two-leg exit (partial close at R1¬∑Œî, trail remainder with `kt¬∑ATR`) is empirically well-justified: it books early profit during the common "pop-and-reverse" crypto pattern while staying in for trend extension. [metrotrade](https://www.metrotrade.com/trend-following-strategy/)

## Critical Weaknesses on Volatile Crypto

### Regime Sensitivity of Dual-MA Trend Filter
The condition \(\text{Bull}_t = \mathbf{1}[C_t > \text{MA}_{200}(t) \wedge \text{MA}_{50}(t) > \text{MA}_{200}(t)]\) is a *lagging confirmation*. In crypto, MA50/MA200 alignment can persist for weeks, then flip violently within 2‚Äì3 bars. Your entries therefore tend to cluster near the *end* of trends rather than the beginning, meaning you're buying strength after the move is partly exhausted. [crypto-trading](https://crypto-trading.ca/post/momentum-vs-mean-reversion-a-regime-switching-playbook-for-canadian-and-global-crypto-traders)

### RSI Filter Conflict With Trend Strength
Requiring `RSI ‚â§ rL` (pullback) **and** `ADX ‚â• a` (strong trend) simultaneously creates a filter paradox in crypto: strong trending assets (ADX high) rarely give deep RSI pullbacks. The practical effect is very low signal frequency ‚Äî which reduces curve-fit risk but also tanks your trade count, triggering the `Penalty(Œ∏) += 1000 ¬∑ 1[Trades < 30]` term and leaving statistical estimates of E(Œ∏) unreliable. [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.10785v1)

### The Broken Metrics Bug Is Non-Trivial
You correctly identified it: your `calc_metrics()` treats each exit leg as an independent trade, which overstates win rate and understates average loss. For a two-leg exit, the partial close at TP1 **always** wins if price hit R1¬∑Œî, so it inflates `win_rate` by ~30‚Äì50% depending on R1/R2 split. The position-level Rp you defined:

\[R_p = \frac{\sum_{\ell \in p} \text{PnL}_{p,\ell}}{\text{InitialRiskUSD}_p}\]

is the only honest metric. Until you fix this, **no reported expectancy number is trustworthy**.

### Parameter Dimensionality and Overfitting Risk
You have 13 free parameters (Œ∏) with a complex, non-smooth objective J(Œ∏) on a regime-switching asset. With 13 dimensions even a small Optuna budget of 200 trials is computationally visiting a space of \(O(10^{13})\) possible combinations. The practical risk: [blog.quantinsti](https://blog.quantinsti.com/walk-forward-optimization-introduction/)

| Risk Factor | Impact |
|---|---|
| 13-parameter space | Very high curve-fit probability |
| Conditional entry logic | Non-convex, lots of local optima |
| Crypto regime shifts (bull ‚Üí bear ‚Üí sideways) | Parameters that work in one regime fail in another |
| Partial-exit bug inflating metrics | Optimizer maximizes a wrong objective |

Walk-forward optimization using Optuna is the correct mitigation, but it only works reliably once the position-level Rp bug is fixed ‚Äî otherwise you're optimizing a noisy, incorrect signal. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UnJTDsrq-Ok)

## What a "Solid Edge" Actually Requires Here

A strategy has a demonstrated edge in volatile crypto if it satisfies all of these simultaneously after walk-forward validation: [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.10785v1)

- **Position-level expectancy E(Œ∏) > 0.10R** consistently across out-of-sample windows (not just in-sample)
- **At least 30+ position-level trades** per walk-forward window (not exit legs)
- **Maximum drawdown < 20‚Äì25%** in out-of-sample periods
- **Parameter stability**: adjacent parameter values (Œ∏ ¬± Œµ) produce similar J(Œ∏), not a sharp peak that collapses immediately ‚Äî that's a sign of overfitting, not edge [blog.quantinsti](https://blog.quantinsti.com/walk-forward-optimization-introduction/)
- **No look-ahead bias** in MA/ATR calculations at entry bar

## Concrete Fixes Before Claiming Edge

1. **Fix `calc_metrics()`** to group legs by `position_id` and compute Rp at position level first
2. **Add a regime filter** (e.g., ATR-percentile or HMM-based) so the strategy pauses during crypto's mean-reversion chop phases, where your trend-follow logic bleeds [crypto-trading](https://crypto-trading.ca/post/momentum-vs-mean-reversion-a-regime-switching-playbook-for-canadian-and-global-crypto-traders)
3. **Constrain parameter search**: fix `R1 ‚àà [1.5, 2.5]`, `R2 ‚àà [3, 5]`, `kc ‚àà [1.5, 2.5]` based on theory rather than optimizing them freely ‚Äî this shrinks the overfitting surface
4. **Walk-forward ratio**: use at least a 3:1 in-sample/out-of-sample split, score only on out-of-sample E(Œ∏), and report the distribution of out-of-sample results ‚Äî not just the best run
